Design Process

Below is my exquisite corpse. I drew the head, Amber drew the body, and Daniel drew the legs. I wanted to create a general abstraction of the exquisite corpse. Given our focus on functions this week, I knew I was going to have 3 distinct functions: drawHead(), drawBody(), and drawLegs(). For the head function, I focused on the shapes, lines, and proportions. For the body and legs, I focused on color and texture, using alphaValues and grayValues. I first started with the head to recreate a head with horns on a figure with an open mouth. It took some experimenting to get the right placement to accurately represent the proportions. Then, I considered the body that Amber drew. It was a rectangular silhouette of a blue man with arms. I chose a darker blue for the body and allowed the arms and outline of the body to be a brighter, lighter blue. I used the documentation and ChatGPT to help me understand how to use curveVertex() to get the proper curves for the arms. After finding the right saturation and angles, I moved onto creating the tail. I used an upside triangle to represent the main body of the figure, and overlayed two triangles to create "fins." Their outline and fill are slightly different colors to help create dimension. To make it a bit more dynamic, I added a bouncing shadow beneath.

Reflection

I think organizing code into separate functions is great! It's much more digestible, especially with well-named functions. I hope to use this going forward in any coding projects. It was fun to make up a story and collaborate with others, and especially interesting to see what kinds of characters we all gravitated towards. Thanks to Mati Pina for sharing helpful references about bezier curves!

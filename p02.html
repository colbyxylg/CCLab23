Project #2: Form Generator

Description

An interactive Cool Cat! Are you cool, too?

Design Process

After drawing my sketches, I began to write all the pseudocode for the cat eyes/nose/ears. I noted some parts of ideas for interaction, like using random colors for the face/ears fill, but wanted to establish my form first. During my coding, I was reminded of the sequential execution: I needed to draw the whites of the eyes first, and then the black irises. After finishing this cat form, I moved on to how I wanted to incorporate interaction with mouseX, mouseY, and mousePress.

I initially wanted a mousePress to trigger a change in position of whiskers. But then I created text based on the location of mouseX and mouseY positions, and I realized that I could say a new message when the mouse was pressed. At first, I wanted the text to replace the initial text but found it was simpler to move the mousePress text to a new position. It was really hard to see to the point I thought my code wasn't working. However, I then realized that it was appearing upon the click of a mouse but it was flashing too quickly. So, I decided to slow down the frame rate when the mouse was pressed. It became a cool freeze frame and a more digestible way of experiencing the interaction. Lastly, because the mousePress text enthusiastically answers the question asked "are you a cool cat," I wanted the cat to reflect it. So, I adjusted the arcs to make a smile. I was stumped at first in adjusting the right hand side of the mouth-- I opened a separate file to play with the paramters, and realized that because the arc bends in the opposite direction, I needed to subtract some radians (rather than add) to achieve a smile. After solving that last problem, I realized I had everything I needed!

Reflection

In reflection, I think it was meaningful and fun to make an interactive graphic. The form of the cat changed by selecting different colors--one thing I discovered is that the cats almost seem to have different emotions with each color. For example, the tan cat feels chill whereas a neon blue or pink cat feels like a party cat. One discovery (not pre-planned) was that it was fun to physically change the cat's expression, by making it smile. I think it was fun using a combination of mouseX/mouseY, and the relative position of mouseX/mouseY, in addition to mousePress to create interaction.

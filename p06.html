Project #6: Data Portrait

Description

Free the Fart! This project explores the time, duration, sound, and strength (smelliness) of a fart in a silly way. Press the mouse repeatedly and you may hear some sounds :)

Design Process

I decided to track my and my loved ones’ farts this last week. I believe that I am a product of my community, and as an effort to embody that, I opened my Google form up to friends. I collected results anonymously (n=17) and estimate that my entries were about half or less. In my Google form, I decided to count each fart and note its time, duration of fart, strength, and sound. I then put these entries into p5: time of fart is denoted by color and text, duration of fart is the dimension of the smokey “fart” particle, the sound of the fart is used to trigger a fart sound, and the strength of the fart is denoted by a wind pattern to the fart, where not smelly is no wind and very smelly is a strong right-blowing wind. I added a small amount of interaction so that a viewer can eagerly click, hoping to hear a silly fart sound.

Reflection

I learned that we fart a lot! I tried my best to record each fart, but know that some did not make it in. An older Vox article suggested we fart 10-20 times a day. The process of tracking this and becoming more aware of my body also made me more aware of my diet. I think the nuance of both how stinky the fart was and the duration of the fart was lost in the data- by representing each fart with overlapping clouds, the individuality of the fart becomes less prominent. Instead, the time of day is now more important. I think this meaningfully reflects the subjectivity of the fart - most people said their farts were not stinky and were silent. In practice, I’m curious how true this actually is. Self-recording leaves a lot up to interpretation! Shoutout to Joy Shen for helping me conceptualize this. Thanks to QuickSounds.com for the sound effect!
